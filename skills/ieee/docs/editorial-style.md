# IEEE Editorial Style and Writing Conventions

Rules from the IEEE Editorial Style Manual for Authors (updated March 2025). Covers article structure, writing style, grammar, and special paper types for IEEE Transactions, Journals, and Letters.

## Article Structure and Ordering

Articles follow this component sequence:

1. **Title page** -- article title, byline, membership citation, first footnote
2. **Abstract** -- single paragraph, 150--250 words, no equations/references/footnotes
3. **Index Terms** -- alphabetical keywords; capitalize first word only; define acronyms
4. **Nomenclature** (optional) -- symbol list before Introduction; no Roman numeral heading
5. **Introduction** -- begins with a drop cap (initial cap, two lines deep) in full-length articles
6. **Body sections**
7. **Conclusion**
8. **Appendix(es)** -- heading not preceded by Roman numeral; plural is "Appendixes"
9. **Acknowledgment** -- singular spelling (no "e" between g and m); never enumerated
10. **References**
11. **Biographies** -- three paragraphs (education, career, memberships/awards)

### Section heading levels

| Level | Name | Format |
|-------|------|--------|
| Primary | section | Roman numeral, centered, ALL CAPS (e.g., `I. INTRODUCTION`) |
| Secondary | subsect1 | Capital letter + period, flush left, italic, title case (e.g., `A. Formal Frameworks`) |
| Tertiary | subsect2 | Arabic numeral + `)`, indented 1 em, italic run-in, title case, followed by colon |
| Quaternary | subsect3 | Lowercase letter + `)`, indented 2 ems, italic run-in, only first word capitalized |

- Introduction, Conclusion, and Acknowledgment are singular headings.
- Reference and Acknowledgment headings are never enumerated.
- In-text references: "in Section II" or "in Section II-A" or "in Section II-A1" (capitalize "Section"; never use "Subsection").
- Drop cap: first letter of the Introduction is a two-line drop cap in full-length articles and editorials (not in short papers). Remaining characters of the first word plus 1--2 additional words are capitalized. Do not break hyphenated words across cap/lowercase.

## Title and Byline Rules

### Title capitalization

Capitalize: nouns, pronouns, adjectives, verbs, adverbs, subordinating conjunctions (If, Because, That, Which), prepositions of more than three letters (Before, From, Through, With, Versus, Among, Under, Between, Without). Capitalize abbreviations that are otherwise lowercase (DC, not dc).

Lowercase: articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but, for, or, nor), most short prepositions -- unless first or last word.

### Byline

- Use the longest, most complete author name; match byline and biography exactly.
- No titles or affiliations in the byline.
- No comma before suffixes: `K. S. Snyder Jr.`, `J. Fortunato III`.
- Membership grades after name: Student Member, Graduate Student Member, Associate Member, Member, Senior Member, Fellow, Life Member, Life Senior Member, Life Fellow, etc.
- Affiliate Members are not listed in byline or biography.

### ORCID

ORCID is requested for all authors and **required for the corresponding author** to submit and access proofs.

### First footnote (three-paragraph structure)

1. **First paragraph**: received/revised/accepted dates, publication dates, financial support, prior conference presentation, corresponding author credit (in italics at end).
2. **Second paragraph**: author affiliations -- department, university/corporation, city, state/province, postal code, country. Country and corresponding author email are required.
3. **Third paragraph**: supplementary material notice and/or color figure notice with DOI link.

All financial support goes in the first footnote, not in the Acknowledgment.

## Writing Conventions

- **American English** throughout (behavior not behaviour, center not centre, polarization not polarisation). Use Merriam-Webster first spelling.
- **No contractions** in technical text (do not, cannot -- not don't, can't). Exception: "don't care" is allowed in specific technical contexts (e.g., TCAD).
- **"Data" is always plural**: "The data were collected..."
- **"Series" is singular** with "a": "A series of tests was run..."
- **Define acronyms** the first time in the Abstract AND the first time in the body text. Write out the full term first, followed by the acronym in parentheses. Acronyms defined in Nomenclature need not be redefined.
- **No trademark symbols**: do not use TM or (R). Capitalize only the first letter of the trademark name. Optionally add a footnote reading "Trademarked." or "Registered trademark." on first occurrence.
- **International date format**: 4 June 2002 (not June 4, 2002). Spell out the month. Does not apply to references.
- **Percentages**: always numeral + % sign. Repeat the % in ranges: 20%--30%.
- **Decimals**: include leading zero when needed for clarity (0.8); omit trailing zeros.
- **Ranges with units**: unit only after the last number unless units differ: `40--50 mm`, but `50 inches to 7 feet`.
- **Numbers**: spell out below 11; use numerals with units; use ordinal words (zeroth, first, nth), not 1st, 2nd.
- **Equation references in text**: use just the number in parentheses -- "in (1)" -- but spell out "Equation" at the start of a sentence.
- **Capitalization**: discourage unnecessary capitalization; only proper names attached to laws/theorems are capitalized (Abel's theorem, Newton's first law). Capitalize Earth when referring to the planet.
- **AI disclosure**: use of AI-generated content must be disclosed in the Acknowledgment section, identifying the AI system and the specific sections where it was used.

## Hyphenation and Compound Modifiers

### Core rule

Compound modifiers are hyphenated **before** the noun they modify but generally **not after**:

- Before: "a well-prepared plan," "a 42-foot-long boat," "a high-frequency signal"
- After: "The plan was well prepared," "His boat was 42 feet long"

### Exceptions -- no hyphen

- After **comparatives/superlatives**: "a higher order equation," "a worst case value," "nearest neighbor method"
- After **-ly adverbs**: "a widely used method" (no hyphen needed)
- **Chemical compounds**: "sodium chloride crystals" (no hyphen)
- **Predicate adjectives**: "...is well known," "...is second order"

### Compound nouns

- One-syllable verb + short adverb often become one word: setup, takeoff, breakup.
- But the verb forms stay separate: set up, break down, read out.
- Two-word compounds: bandwidth, bypass, flowchart, phase shift, sideband, standing wave.

### En dash (--) usage

Represents "to," "through," or "and." Use between:

- Page numbers: pp. 10--15
- Year ranges: 1984--1990
- Proper-noun pairs: Jones--Smith theorem
- Opposites/pairs: input--output, voltage--current curve, analog--digital converter
- Value ranges: 10--20 cm
- Chemical abbreviations: Ni--Al--Si
- Alloys and mixtures: He--Ne laser

When "from" is used, write "from X to Y" (not "from X--Y").

### Em dash

Used for parenthetical asides or suspension of sense within a sentence.

Book review titles separate title from author with an em dash.

## Grammar and Usage

### Serial (Oxford) comma -- required

In a series of three or more terms, always place a comma before the conjunction:
"cables, transformers, etc., are needed."

### That vs. which

- **"That"** introduces restrictive (defining) clauses -- no commas: "The proof that is given in this section..."
- **"Which"** introduces nonrestrictive (parenthetical) clauses -- commas required: "The address i, which is the starting address, is then transferred..."

### Semicolons

- Link two independent clauses with no connecting word.
- Join independent clauses with conjunctive adverbs (however, moreover, therefore, consequently, nevertheless, thus).
- Use a colon after an independent clause to introduce a list.

### Punctuation with quotation marks

Punctuation goes **inside** quotation marks, **except** for colons and semicolons. Use single quotation marks for quotes within quotes.

### Parentheses

Do not use double parentheses in text. Replace `(see (10))` with `[see (10)]`. Double parentheses are acceptable in math expressions.

### Possessives

- Add 's for singular nouns: Avogadro's theorem.
- Exception: if the final consonant is "s," use only the apostrophe: Burns' theorem.
- Possessive pronouns have no apostrophe: its, hers, yours, theirs.
- Plural acronyms/years have no apostrophe: FETs, 1980s.

### Commas between adjectives

Do not use commas between adjectives: "a planar equiangular spiral antenna."

### Commonly confused words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **affect** (v.) | to change or modify |
| **effect** (n.) | result; (v.) to cause |
| **alternate** | a substitute |
| **alternative** | a matter of choice |
| **among** | involves more than two things |
| **between** | considers items individually (even if more than two) |
| **compare to** | point out resemblances between different objects |
| **compare with** | point out similarities and differences between same objects |
| **compose** | to make up: "a set composed of members" |
| **comprise** | to be made up of: "a set comprising members" |
| **farther** | distance |
| **further** | quantity or degree |
| **fewer** | countable items: "fewer tubes" |
| **less** | uncountable mass/abstract: "less air" |
| **imply** | suggest though not express |
| **infer** | deduce from evidence |
| **number** | countable: "a large number of people" |
| **amount** | uncountable: "a large amount of water" |
| **principal** (adj.) | chief, main |
| **principle** (n.) | a rule |
| **precede** | come before |
| **proceed** | continue, advance |
| **that** | restrictive / defining |
| **which** | nonrestrictive / nondefining |

## Figures and Tables

### Figures

- Always abbreviate as **"Fig."** followed by the figure number -- even at the start of a sentence.
- Caption format: `Fig. 1.` followed by an em space, then caption text. Capitalize the first word.
- Do not begin captions with A, An, or The.
- First citations of all figures and tables must appear in numerical order in the text.
- Figure footnotes are placed as part of the caption.
- Parts labeled (a), (b), etc. in the caption; cite with singular "Fig." even for multiple parts: "Fig. 1(a) and 1(b)."

### Tables

- Heading centered above the table: `TABLE I` (Roman numerals, caps).
- Descriptive caption centered below the table number, no trailing period.
- Style table captions as an inverted pyramid.

### Lena image ban

IEEE will no longer accept papers containing the "Lena image" (effective 1 April 2024).

### Permission/copyright reuse

- Reusing IEEE graphics: request permission from IEEE Xplore; give full credit to original source; add reference number at end of caption.
- Reusing non-IEEE graphics: obtain advance permission from the copyright holder (usually the publisher, not the author); add supplied credit text at end of caption.

## Special Paper Types

### Editorials

- No Abstract.
- Byline does not appear below the title; author signature appears at the end with affiliation.
- Acknowledgment has no heading and need not be written in third person.
- Citations to articles in the issue are listed as "Related Works" instead of in the References.
- Title may be simply "Editorial," "Guest Editorial," or article type + colon + descriptive title.

### Brief papers

- Include Abstract and initial cap (drop cap).
- Byline includes membership grade.
- No biographies or author photographs.

### Short papers, letters, correspondence, communications

- Formatted like full-length articles.
- Membership grade is **not** included in the byline.
- No biographies or author photographs.
- Letters have strict low page limits and appear in a back section.

### Comments and replies

- Comments respond to a previously published article.
- Must include Abstracts and Index Terms.
- Begin the first sentence with: `In the above article [1], ...`
- Reference [1] is the commented article's citation.
- Title format: `Comments on "Original Article Title"`
- Reply title: `Authors' Reply`

### Corrections / errata / addendums

- **Correction**: author error. **Erratum**: publisher error. **Addendum**: supplementary statement.
- Abstracts and Index Terms are optional but must be both present or both absent.
- Use plural form in the title even for a single correction.
- Byline must match the original article exactly (ensures proper linking).
- Title formats:
  - `Corrections to "Original Article Title"`
  - `Erratum to "Original Article Title"`
  - `Addendum to "Original Article Title"`
- IEEE does not republish the original article with changes; only the correction/erratum is published.

### Book reviews

- Same format as short paper / correspondence.
- Title separated from book author by an em dash.
- Parenthetical: city, publisher, year, page count, price.
- Reviewer name in italics.
- Example: `The Analysis and Design of Pneumatic Systems---B. L. Andersen. (New York: Wiley, 1987, 302 pp., $65.00.) Reviewed by J. L. Shearer.`
- Footnote uses "The reviewer is with..." (not "The author is with...").

## Inclusive Language

IEEE requires inclusive, people-first language. Key replacements:

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| blacklist / black list | blocklist / block list |
| whitelist / white list | access list |
| white box | glass box |
| black box | closed box |
| master / slave | primary / secondary (preferred); leader / follower; parent / child |
| man hours / man weeks | work hours / work weeks |
| manpower | workforce, labor force |
| manned | crewed, piloted, human |
| unmanned | autonomous, uncrewed, remote |
| unmanned aerial vehicle | autonomous aerial vehicle (AAV) |
| chairman / chairwoman | chairperson |
| single-blind / double-blind | single-anonymous / double-anonymous |
| A.D. | C.E. (common era) |
| B.C. | B.C.E. (before common era) |

Additional guidance:

- Use people-first language: "person with X," "has been diagnosed with X."
- Do not spell out the acronym LGBTQIA+.
- Always use a transgender person's chosen name and pronouns.
- Defer to the pronoun provided by the author; treat singular "they/them" as non-binary singular pronouns.
