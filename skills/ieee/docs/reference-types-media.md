# IEEE Reference Types: Media and Online Sources

This document covers 7 media/online reference categories from the IEEE Reference
Style Guide: Blogs, Books, Datasets, News Articles, Online Video, Software, and
Websites.

---

## Blogs (A)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of the post," Title of the Blog, Month, Day, Year. [Online]. Available: URL`

**Subtypes:** single-author blog post, multi-author blog post

**Required Fields:** author, post title, blog title, date (month day year), URL
**Optional Fields:** (none)

**Examples:**

[1] A. Weissberger, "FCC approves EchoStar/Dish request to extend timeline for its 5G buildout," IEEE ComSoc Technol. Blog, Sep. 23, 2024. [Online]. Available: https://techblog.comsoc.org/2024/09/23/fcc-approves-echostar-dish-request-to-extend-timeline-for-its-5g-buildout/

[2] M. Owens, "How two Cisco women leaned in and supercharged their careers," Cisco Blogs, Jun. 23, 2023. [Online]. Available: https://blogs.cisco.com/diversity/how-two-cisco-women-leaned-in-and-supercharged-their-careers

**Notes:**
- Blog title is the name of the blog itself (e.g., "IEEE ComSoc Technol. Blog"), not the hosting site.
- Always ends with `[Online]. Available: URL` (no period after URL).
- Date uses abbreviated month format (e.g., "Sep." not "September").

## Books (B)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, xth ed. City of Publisher, (only U.S. State), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Subtypes:** basic book, online book/monograph, foreign/translated book, book with chapter title, book with editor(s), book with series title/volume/edition

**Required Fields:** author(s), book title, city of publisher, country, publisher abbreviation, year
**Optional Fields:** chapter title, edition, chapter number, section number, page range, volume, series title, editor(s), translator, URL (for online), accessed date (for online)

### Basic Book

**Examples:**

[1] B. Klaus and P. Horn, Robot Vision. Cambridge, MA, USA: MIT Press, 1986.

[2] L. Stein, "Random patterns," in Computers and You, J. S. Brake, Ed., New York, NY, USA: Wiley, 1994, pp. 55-70.

### Book With Chapter Title

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, X. Editor, Ed., City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Examples:**

[3] T. Ogura, "Electronic government and surveillance-oriented society," in Theorizing Surveillance: The Panopticon and Beyond. Cullompton, U.K.: Willan, 2006, ch. 13, pp. 270-295.

### Book With Editor(s)

**Basic Format (chapter in edited book):**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, X. Editor, Ed., City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Basic Format (entire edited book):**
`[#] X. Editor, Ed. Title of Published Book. City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year.`

**Examples:**

[4] C. Bennett, "What happens when you book an airline ticket? The collection and processing of passenger data post-9/11," in Global Surveillance and Policing: Borders, Security, Identity, E. Zureik and M. Salter, Eds., Cullompton, U.K.: Willan, 2005, pp. 113-138.

[5] W. R. Leonard and M. H. Crawford, Eds. Human Biology of Pastoral Populations. New York, NY, USA: Cambridge Univ. Press, 2002.

### Books, Monographs (Online)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, xth ed. City of Publisher, State, Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx. [Online]. Available: URL`

**Examples:**

[6] G. O. Young, "Synthetic structure of industrial plastics," in Plastics, vol. 3, Polymers of Hexadromicon, J. Peters, Ed., 2nd ed. New York, NY, USA: McGraw-Hill, 1964, pp. 15-64. [Online]. Available: https://www.scirp.org

[7] The Terahertz Wave eBook. ZOmega Terahertz Corp., 2014. Accessed: May 19, 2014. [Online]. Available: https://www.scribd.com/document/322662319/Thz-zomega-ebook-pdf-1206-sr-pdf

### Book (Foreign Publication or Translated)

**Basic Format (untranslated foreign publication):**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, X. Editor, Ed., xth ed. City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher (in Language), year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Basic Format (translated publication):**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book, trans. J. K. Translator. City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Examples:**

[8] K. Ichiro, Thai Economy and Railway 1885-1935, Tokyo: Nihon Keizai Hyoronsha (in Japanese), 2000.

[9] J.-L. Dessalles, Why We Talk: The Evolutionary Origins of Language, trans. James Grieve. Oxford, U.K.: Oxford Univ. Press, 2007.

### Book With Series Title, Volume Title, and Edition

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of chapter in the book," in Title of Published Book (Title of the Series), X. Editor, Ed., xth ed. City of Publisher, State (only U.S.), Country: Abbrev. of Publisher, year, ch. x, sect. x, pp. xxx-xxx.`

**Examples:**

[10] M. Feistauer, "Irrotational Incompressible Flow," in Mathematical Methods in Fluid Dynamics (Monographs and Surveys in Pure and Applied Mathematics Series, 67). Harlow, U.K.: Longman, 1993, ch. 2, pp. 77-186.

[11] E. F. Moore, "Gedanken-experiments on sequential machines," in Automata Studies (Ann. of Math. Studies, no. 1), C. E. Shannon and J. McCarthy, Eds., Princeton, NJ, USA: Princeton Univ. Press, 1965, pp. 129-153.

**Notes:**
- For U.S. publishers, include the two-letter state abbreviation (e.g., "MA, USA").
- For non-U.S. publishers, omit state; use country abbreviation (e.g., "U.K.").
- Use "Ed." for one editor, "Eds." for multiple editors.
- Chapter titles are quoted; book titles are italicized (in typeset output).
- When a chapter title is present, the book title is preceded by "in".
- For corporate authors, spell out the full organization name.
- Online books append `[Online]. Available: URL` and optionally `Accessed: date.`
- For foreign works, append `(in Language)` after the publisher name.
- For translated works, add `trans. Translator Name.` after the book title.

## Datasets (E)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] Author, Date, Year. "Title of Dataset," distributed by Publisher/Distributor, URL`

**Subtypes:** basic dataset, dataset with DOI, dataset with DOI resolver, dataset with website address

**Required Fields:** author(s), date, title of dataset, publisher/distributor or source
**Optional Fields:** DOI, URL, version number, edition

### Basic Dataset

**Examples:**

[1] S. Ansolabehere, M. Palmer, and A. Lee, January 20, 2014, "Precinct-Level Election Data. V1," distributed by Harvard Election Data Archive, http://hdl.handle.net/1902.1/21919 UNF:5:5C9UfGjdLy2ONVPtgr45qA==

### Dataset Using a DOI

**Basic Format:**
`[#] Author, Date, "Title of Dataset," Source, doi: xxx.`

**Examples:**

[2] U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Aug. 2013, "Treatment Episode Dataset: Discharges (TEDS-D): Concatenated, 2006 to 2009," U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Office of Applied Studies, doi: 10.3886/ICPSR30122.v2.

### Dataset Using a DOI Resolver

**Basic Format:**
`[#] Author, Date, "Title of Dataset," Source, doi: URL.`

**Examples:**

[3] U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Aug. 2013, "Treatment Episode Dataset: Discharges (TEDS-D): Concatenated, 2006 to 2009," U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Office of Applied Studies, doi: http://dx.doi.org/10.3886/ICPSR30122.v2.

### Dataset Using a Website Address

**Basic Format:**
`[#] Author, Date, "Title of Dataset," Source. [Online]. Available: URL`

**Examples:**

[4] U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Aug. 2013, "Treatment Episode Dataset: Discharges (TEDS-D): Concatenated, 2006 to 2009," U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Office of Applied Studies. [Online]. Available: http://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/SAMHDA/studies/30122/version/

**Notes:**
- IEEE follows the FORCE11 Data Citation Principles for datasets.
- Essential citation components: author names, date published/disseminated, complete title (including edition/version), publisher/distributor, and electronic location (URL or DOI).
- When using a DOI, end the reference with a period after the DOI.
- When using a URL (no DOI), do not place a period after the URL.
- Append the date retrieved if the title and locator are not specific to the exact data instance used.
- Provide as much information as available: author(s), name, unique identifier, location/repository, release date, license, description, keywords.

## News Articles (J)

**Basic Format (Print):**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of the article," Title of the News Source, Month, Day, Year.`

**Basic Format (Online):**
`[#] J. K. Author, "Title of the article," Title of the News Source, Month, Day, Year. [Online]. Available: URL`

**Subtypes:** print news article, online news article

**Required Fields:** author, article title, news source title, date (month day year)
**Optional Fields:** URL (required for online variant)

**Examples:**

[1] J. Markoff, "Software is smart enough for SAT, but still far from intelligent," Buffalo News, Sep. 21, 2015.

[2] A. Clark, "A new AI tool creates hyperrealistic photos. Can you tell the difference?" CBS News, Aug. 30, 2024. [Online]. Available: https://www.cbsnews.com/news/can-you-tell-real-image-from-ai-flux/

[3] W. Knight, "The most capable open source AI model yet could supercharge AI agents," Wired, Sep. 25, 2024. [Online]. Available: https://www.wired.com/story/molmo-open-source-multimodal-ai-model-allen-institute-agents/

**Notes:**
- News source title is abbreviated per IEEE conventions (e.g., standard periodical abbreviations).
- Online variant appends `[Online]. Available: URL` with no period after URL.
- Print variant ends with a period after the date.

## Online Video (K)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] Video Owner/Creator, Location (if available). Title of Video: In Initial Caps. (Release date). Accessed: Month Day, Year. [Online Video]. Available: URL`

**Subtypes:** online video (e.g., YouTube)

**Required Fields:** video owner/creator, video title, release date, accessed date, URL
**Optional Fields:** location

**Examples:**

[1] mtaOnline1, Fazi Mosque, U.K. An Occasionally Accurate History of Australia: Part I. (Oct. 23, 2006). Accessed: Oct. 6, 2010. [Online Video]. Available: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IJjNsCVHc34

[2] Doane Academy, Burlington, NJ, USA. Second Grade Bossy R. (Feb. 28, 2013). Accessed: Jun. 3, 2018. [Online Video]. Available: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PUKHo1Y-BcM

**Notes:**
- Video title uses initial caps (title case), not sentence case.
- The tag is `[Online Video]` (not `[Online]`).
- Location follows the creator name, separated by a comma.
- Release date is in parentheses.
- Always include an accessed date.

## Software (O)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] J. K. Author. Title of Software. Date Repository or Archive. (version or year). Publisher Name. Accessed: Date (when applicable). [Type of Medium]. Global Persistent Identifier. Available: site/path/file`

**Subtypes:** software product, software archive/repository, software package (mixed data/software)

**Required Fields:** author(s) or organization, software title, version or year, publisher name
**Optional Fields:** repository/archive, accessed date, medium type, global persistent identifier, URL, release date, license, description, keywords

**Examples:**

[1] Antenna Products. (2011). Antcom. Accessed: January 11, 2019. [Online]. Available: https://www.antcom.com/products

[2] D. W. Arning et al. Mixed Mode-Mixed Level Circuit Simulator. (2011). Ngspice. Accessed: Jan. 11, 2019. [Online]. Available: http://ngspice.sourceforge.net

[3] MSDN Library Visual Studio 6.0. (2001). Microsoft.

**Notes:**
- IEEE follows the FORCE11 Software Citation Principles.
- The amount of detail depends on the citation context. FORCE11 identifies these use cases with recommended metadata:
  - Use software for papers: author(s), software name, location/repository, version number, release date, unique identifier.
  - Use software in/with new software: add software license and description.
  - Contribute to software: add contributor role.
  - Reproduce analysis: software name, location/repository, version number, release date, unique identifier, software license, description.
  - Published mixed data/software package: author(s), software name, unique identifier, location/repository, release date, software license, description, keywords.
- Italicized metadata in the FORCE11 guidelines indicates fields that benefit from inclusion but are not strictly required for the reference listing.
- When an accessed date is applicable (e.g., online software), include it.
- The medium tag is typically `[Online]` for web-accessible software.

## Websites (T)

**Basic Format:**
`[#] First Name Initial(s) Last Name. "Page Title." Website Title. Accessed: Date. [Online]. Available: URL`

**Subtypes:** basic website, social media post

**Required Fields:** author(s), page title, website title, accessed date, URL
**Optional Fields:** (none for basic; social media uses same fields)

### Basic Website

**Examples:**

[1] J. Smith. "Obama inaugurated as President." CNN.com. Accessed: Feb. 1, 2009. [Online.] Available: http://www.cnn.com/POLITICS/01/21/obama_inaugurated/index.html

[2] J. Smith and J. Doe. "Obama inaugurated as President." CNN.com. Accessed: Feb. 1, 2009. [Online.] Available: http://www.cnn.com/POLITICS/01/21/obama_inaugurated/index.html

### Social Media

**Examples:**

[3] P. Costanzo, "Clemson orange uniforms are messing with the color on my TV. And apparently messing with the ND offense, too." Twitter. Accessed Apr. 19, 2021. [Online.] Available: https://twitter.com/PaulCostanzo/status/817826295707041793

**Notes:**
- Author names use first initial(s) then last name, followed by a period.
- Omit titles and affiliations associated with the author.
- Do not use commas before suffixes (Jr./Sr., roman numerals) after the author's given name.
- For multiple authors, list in order as they appear on the website, separated by commas.
- Page title is in quotation marks; website title is not.
- The accessed date format is "Accessed: Abbrev. month day, year."
- The tag is `[Online.]` (note the period inside the bracket for websites).
- No period after the URL.
- Social media posts follow the same format; the post text serves as the page title.
